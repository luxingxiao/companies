K-Dense Science Lab is a comprehensive scientific research institute built on 177 specialized skills from the [claude-scientific-skills](https://github.com/K-Dense-AI/claude-scientific-skills) platform. It operates as a hub-and-spoke organization with a CEO and Chief Science Officer at the center, 11 department directors managing domain-specific teams, and over 50 specialist researchers executing hands-on scientific work.

The lab covers an unusually broad scientific mandate. Its Bioinformatics & Genomics department handles everything from single-cell RNA-seq to phylogenetic inference. Drug Discovery runs virtual screening campaigns and molecular dynamics simulations. Clinical Research interprets genomic variants for precision medicine and processes medical imaging. The Machine Learning team builds models ranging from Bayesian inference to reinforcement learning agents. Physical Sciences spans quantum circuits, fluid dynamics, and materials design.

Supporting these core research departments, the Scientific Databases team provides curated access to 37 databases — from PubMed and UniProt to ChEMBL and the GWAS Catalog. The Data Analysis & Visualization team produces publication-quality figures and statistical analyses. Scientific Communication handles manuscript writing, slide design, and document production. Laboratory Operations connects computational work to physical lab instruments through robotics integrations and cloud lab platforms.

Two additional departments round out the institute. Research Methodology ensures scientific rigor through hypothesis generation, critical analysis, and counterfactual reasoning. Financial Research provides economic context for biotech market intelligence and funding decisions. Together, these 11 departments form a self-contained research organization capable of tackling problems that span multiple scientific disciplines.

## How Work Flows

1. **CEO** receives research requests and routes them to the Chief Science Officer
2. **Chief Science Officer** determines which departments are needed and coordinates cross-departmental projects
3. **Department Leads** (11 departments) manage their specialists and ensure quality
4. **Specialists** (~50 researchers) execute the work using their domain-specific skills
